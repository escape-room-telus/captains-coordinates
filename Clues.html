Clue 6: Rhyme of the Forgotten Voyage

A tattered page from an old sea journal was wedged between the helm and the bulkhead. It appears to be a poem – perhaps nonsense, or perhaps... a clue.

From mast to moon, the winds did scream,
A lonely star marked out our dream.
The sky was clear at latitude four,
And one pale gull above did soar.

The water dark, the compass spun,
We drifted west as day was done.
The helmsman cried, “By heavens bright,
Seventy-one we sail this night!”

The old man’s map was stained and torn,
But in his notes a course was sworn:
“A single point, a northern line,
One less than forty and the divine.”

A whisper passed, a sailor’s breath,
A bearing found in face of death.
They set their sights and prayed for sun,
And turned their prow to forty-one.
